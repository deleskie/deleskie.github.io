## Purpose

This is the internal companion to the customer ceremony audio checklist. The public asset tells couples what to look for. This document defines what staff should confirm before saying ceremony audio is planned.

## Confirm The Space

- Ceremony location name.
- Indoor or outdoor.
- Guest count.
- Approximate aisle and seating layout.
- Distance from ceremony position to back row.
- Nearby noise sources.
- Power availability.
- Rain-plan location.
- Setup and strike timing.

## Confirm People And Inputs

- Officiant.
- Couple vows.
- Readers.
- Singer or musician.
- Planner cue point.
- Any family blessing or prayer.

## Confirm Music

- Prelude source.
- Processional order.
- Processional songs.
- Ceremony moment songs.
- Recessional song.
- Post-ceremony transition.

## Confirm Gear Logic

- Officiant mic.
- Handheld mic if needed.
- Vow pickup strategy.
- Speaker placement.
- Mixer or ceremony control point.
- Battery plan.
- Backup mic path.
- Cable/power safety.

## Confirm Communication

- Who cues ceremony music?
- Who tells us the wedding party is lined up?
- Who approves last-minute ceremony location changes?
- Who communicates rain-plan activation?
- Who controls guest seating timing?

## Staff Standard

Do not treat ceremony audio as "just one speaker and a mic." The ceremony is often the most emotionally important sound moment of the day. If guests cannot hear vows, the rest of the reception cannot fix that.
