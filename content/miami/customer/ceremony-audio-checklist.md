## Why Ceremony Audio Matters

Guests remember whether they could hear the vows. In Miami, ceremonies often happen near water, on terraces, outside hotels, or in spaces with wind and ambient noise. Ceremony audio needs its own plan, even when the reception is nearby.

## People Who May Need Audio

- Officiant.
- Couple.
- Readers.
- Family blessing or prayer.
- Singer or live musician.
- Planner or coordinator making a cue.

## Music Cues To Confirm

- Guest seating music.
- Wedding party processional.
- Partner processional.
- Ceremony moment music, if any.
- Recessional.
- Transition music after the ceremony.

## Location Details

- Is the ceremony indoors, outdoors, rooftop, terrace, beach, lawn, or ballroom?
- How many guests will be seated?
- How far is the back row from the ceremony spot?
- Is there nearby traffic, wind, water, restaurant noise, or public activity?
- Is power available at the ceremony location?
- Is a separate ceremony setup required?
- Is there a rain plan or backup room?

## Microphone Planning

- Officiant mic.
- Vow pickup.
- Handheld mic for readings.
- Handheld or stand mic for singers.
- Fresh batteries.
- Backup mic path.

## Speaker Planning

- Speakers should cover guests, not blast the couple.
- Speaker placement should avoid blocking photos.
- Volume should be tested from the back rows.
- Ceremony sound should be clean and natural, not harsh.

## Questions To Ask Your DJ

- Do you provide a separate ceremony system?
- Can you support vows clearly?
- How do you handle wind or outdoor noise?
- Where will speakers be placed?
- Do you coordinate ceremony cues with the planner?
- What happens if the ceremony moves indoors?
- How early do you arrive for setup and sound check?

## What To Send Before Event Day

- Ceremony location.
- Processional song list.
- Recessional song.
- Officiant name.
- Reader names.
- Any pronunciation notes.
- Venue rules.
- Planner or ceremony coordinator contact.
- Rain-plan location.

## Bottom Line

If the ceremony matters, audio cannot be an afterthought. Plan it as a separate moment with microphones, music cues, speaker coverage, power, and a backup-minded setup.
