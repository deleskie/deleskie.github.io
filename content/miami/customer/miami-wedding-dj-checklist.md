## What This Helps You Avoid

A wedding DJ is not only there to play songs. The right DJ helps protect the sound, timing, announcements, formal moments, and dance-floor energy of the day. Use this checklist to compare DJs by preparation, not just price or playlist.

## Before You Book

- Confirm the DJ is available for your exact date, location, and event hours.
- Ask who will actually be present on event day.
- Ask whether MC service is included or separate.
- Ask how ceremony audio is handled if the ceremony is in a different space.
- Ask whether wireless microphones are included for vows, officiant, speeches, and toasts.
- Ask what happens if the venue has strict load-in, power, or noise rules.
- Ask how they prepare names, introductions, family formalities, and timeline cues.
- Ask how music planning is collected and confirmed before the wedding.
- Ask whether lighting, uplighting, or dance lighting is available if the room needs atmosphere.
- Ask what backup plan exists for key audio gear.

## Ceremony Sound

- Officiant mic planned.
- Couple vows planned.
- Reader or singer mic planned, if needed.
- Ceremony music cues documented.
- Speaker placement reviewed for guest coverage.
- Power source confirmed.
- Wind, water, or outdoor noise considered.
- Rain or room-flip plan discussed.

## Reception Flow

- Wedding party names and pronunciation collected.
- Grand entrance order confirmed.
- First dance, parent dances, speeches, and cake timing reviewed.
- Dinner music style selected.
- Open dance floor timing planned.
- Last song or final private moment discussed.
- Planner, venue, photographer, and catering timing considered.

## Music

- Must-play songs collected.
- Do-not-play songs collected.
- Guest request policy agreed on.
- Explicit music preferences discussed.
- Latin, classics, current hits, and family favorites balanced.
- Cultural or family traditions documented.

## Green Flags

- The DJ asks detailed questions before giving a final plan.
- The DJ talks about ceremony, MC flow, and timeline, not just dancing.
- The DJ can explain how announcements will feel in the room.
- The DJ understands venue logistics.
- The DJ gives you a clear next step after the inquiry.

## Red Flags

- No questions about ceremony audio.
- No process for pronunciation or family names.
- No discussion of timeline or planner coordination.
- Vague answers about backup gear.
- Pressure to book before the scope is clear.

## Best Next Step

Share your date, venue, ceremony location, guest count, must-have moments, and any bilingual or family traditions. A strong DJ should use those details to recommend the right setup, not force every event into the same package.
