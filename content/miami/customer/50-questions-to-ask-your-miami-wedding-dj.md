## How To Use These Questions

You do not need to ask every question on a call. Use them to understand whether a DJ is thinking about your whole event: sound, timing, announcements, family moments, music, venue logistics, and the guest experience.

## Availability And Fit

1. Are you available for our date?
2. Who will be the DJ and MC at our wedding?
3. How many weddings or private events do you usually handle in a weekend?
4. What types of Miami events are the best fit for your style?
5. How do you decide what setup our event needs?

## Ceremony Audio

6. Do you provide ceremony audio?
7. Can you mic the officiant?
8. Can you support vows if we want guests to hear every word?
9. What happens if the ceremony is outside?
10. How do you handle wind, water, distance, or traffic noise?
11. Do you handle ceremony music cues?
12. Can you support a reader, singer, or musician?
13. Do you need power at the ceremony site?
14. What is your rain-plan process?
15. Is ceremony audio a separate setup from reception audio?

## MC And Timeline

16. Is MC service included?
17. How do you handle grand entrances?
18. How do you collect name pronunciations?
19. Can announcements be made in English and Spanish?
20. How do you coordinate with the planner or venue?
21. How do you keep speeches from feeling awkward or rushed?
22. How do you handle last-minute timeline changes?
23. Will you have our final timeline before event day?
24. Do you create or review an event packet?
25. How do you keep formal moments organized without over-talking?

## Music Planning

26. How do you collect must-play songs?
27. How do you collect do-not-play songs?
28. How many must-play songs are realistic?
29. How do you handle guest requests?
30. Can we choose genres instead of every song?
31. How do you balance older guests and younger guests?
32. How do you handle Latin music, classics, and current hits?
33. What do you do if the dance floor starts slowly?
34. Can we give you songs for dinner, cocktail hour, and dancing?
35. Do you take explicit lyric preferences seriously?

## Production And Venue Details

36. What equipment do you bring?
37. Do you bring wireless microphones?
38. Do you bring backup gear?
39. How much time do you need for load-in?
40. What do you need from the venue?
41. Do you need a table, linen, or power?
42. Can you work with hotel or rooftop venue rules?
43. Do you carry insurance if the venue requires it?
44. Do you offer lighting or uplighting?
45. How do you keep the setup clean and appropriate for the room?

## Trust And Communication

46. What happens after we book?
47. When do we have final planning conversations?
48. How do we reach you with updates?
49. What is the final deadline for music and timeline details?
50. What should we prepare before our first planning call?

## What A Good Answer Sounds Like

The best answers are specific. You should hear how the DJ protects the ceremony, manages the room, coordinates with vendors, respects family details, and builds energy without turning the wedding into a generic show.
