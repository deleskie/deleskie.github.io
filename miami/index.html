FAQ

Miami event questions, answered clearly.

No inflated claims, no fake venue lists, and no vague promises. These are the practical details that matter before you book.

Do you provide ceremony audio?

Yes. Ceremony audio can include dedicated speakers, wireless microphones, processional music, and a separate setup when the ceremony and reception are in different spaces.

Can you support bilingual events?

Yes. The planning intake captures bilingual MC needs, pronunciation notes, family moments, music balance, and cultural details so the flow is prepared instead of improvised.

Do you work with planners and venues?

Yes. The workflow is planner-friendly: timeline coordination, load-in awareness, power notes, rain-plan context, and a concise event packet.

Do you handle outdoor events?

Outdoor events can be supported when power, coverage, weather, and backup-location details are clear. Beach and outdoor ceremonies should include rain or cover notes.

Do you bring backup equipment?

Backup equipment planning is part of the production mindset. The final gear list depends on the package, room, ceremony needs, and event scope.

How far in advance should we book?

For weddings, quinceaneras, and hotel events, 3 to 12 months is a useful planning window. Shorter timelines may still be possible depending on availability.

Do you offer lighting?

Yes. Lighting can include room atmosphere, dance-floor movement, and production support. The goal is a polished Miami feel without overwhelming the room.

Do you do corporate and hotel events?

Yes. Corporate, hotel, brand, and private events can be quoted as custom production plans with DJ/MC, sound, lighting, and planner coordination.
