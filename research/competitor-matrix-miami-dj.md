# TC Audio Productions Miami Competitor Matrix

## Methodology

This is a first-pass public-source competitive intelligence pack for the Miami DJ, MC, wedding, quinceanera, corporate event, and event-production market. It uses public competitor websites, public vendor/review profiles where accessible, and official Google documentation for review-access constraints.

No competitor copy, testimonials, reviews, images, awards, or claims should be reused by TC Audio Productions. Findings below are converted into strategic patterns only.

Google review note: Google Places API Place Details can return rating/review information, but Google's Place resource documents a maximum of five reviews for a place. For reviews on an owned Google Business Profile, Google's Business Profile APIs are the correct route for listing and managing owned-location reviews.

Sources:
- Google Places API Place resource: https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/reference/rest/v1/places
- Google Business Profile reviews: https://developers.google.com/my-business/content/review-data
- Google hreflang guidance: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/specialty/international/localized-versions
- Digital.gov multilingual access guidance: https://digital.gov/resources/creating-multilingual-websites/

## Strategic Gaps TC Can Own

1. Smart event intake is underdeveloped across the competitor set. Most competitors still use quote forms, contact pages, or simple availability forms.
2. Spanish content is often either absent, mixed into English pages, or SEO-dense. A polished es-US editorial lane can be a real moat.
3. Many competitors claim energy, packed dance floors, or premium service. Fewer explain how they prevent ceremony audio issues, timeline confusion, bad MC flow, playlist mistakes, and vendor coordination problems.
4. Package cards often sell gear or generic service bundles. TC should sell outcomes: clean ceremony, bilingual flow, planner-ready packet, controlled guest requests, backup mindset, and lighting atmosphere.
5. Corporate/event-production competitors show technical breadth, but wedding-focused competitors often have warmer conversion copy. TC should combine both.
