# Review Theme Summary: Miami DJ and Event Production

## Review Access Boundaries

This pack does not scrape restricted review platforms or reuse competitor review text. It mines public review-profile patterns and site-owned testimonial themes at a strategic level.

Google review collection should be handled through compliant APIs:

- Google Places API Place resource, including review/rating fields and the documented five-review maximum: https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/reference/rest/v1/places
- Google Business Profile review APIs for owned locations: https://developers.google.com/my-business/content/review-data

## Positive Themes Buyers Reward

| Theme | What Buyers Are Really Saying | TC Website Translation |
|---|---|---|
| Responsiveness | Couples and planners want fast answers before they trust the vendor. | Put fast-response expectations near every form and CTA. |
| Organization | Clients praise planning portals, forms, timelines, and preparation. | Make the smart event profile the centerpiece, not a generic contact form. |
| Playlist control | Buyers care about must-play, do-not-play, family songs, and guest requests. | Add intake questions for music balance, guest-request policy, and do-not-play rules. |
| Reading the room | Clients remember whether the dance floor stayed active without force. | Sell professional flow and crowd reading, not loud hype. |
| Bilingual MC | Miami families need English/Spanish announcements handled naturally. | Make bilingual MC a first-class service and Spanish page lane. |
| Cultural music | Latin weddings and quinces need salsa, merengue, bachata, reggaeton, classics, and crossover handled with taste. | Add Latin-first planning sections and Spanish blog content. |
| Ceremony audio | Bad ceremony sound is a major anxiety point. | Explain wireless mics, separate ceremony space planning, power, wind, and backup path. |
| Planner coordination | Planners want early arrival, clean load-in, one point of contact, and timeline discipline. | Add a planner/venue-friendly module and event packet promise. |
| Not cheesy | Premium buyers reject gimmicky DJ behavior and excessive talking. | Offer MC-style preferences: minimal, polished, warm, bilingual, energetic, corporate. |
| Lighting impact | Buyers value room transformation when it feels intentional. | Show lighting as atmosphere design, not just colored lights. |

## Negative Themes to Prevent

These are common buyer-risk patterns across DJ/event review ecosystems. They should be converted into TC process safeguards rather than competitor-specific claims.

| Risk Theme | Website Safeguard |
|---|---|
| Slow response or unclear next step | Show "what happens next" under every form: review details, confirm availability, recommend setup, schedule consult. |
| Ignored playlist or do-not-play list | Build structured music preferences and do-not-play fields into the smart intake. |
| Wrong song or awkward formalities | Add pronunciation guide, key-song fields, formalities checklist, and final event packet. |
| Too much talking or cheesy MC | Add MC-style selector and language preference. |
| Poor ceremony sound | Add ceremony audio detail, separate ceremony/reception setup logic, and outdoor/rain-plan fields. |
| Hidden add-ons | Package cards should clearly label quote-based services and optional add-ons. |
| Unprepared replacement or backup issue | Explain backup gear mindset without fearmongering. |
| Disorganized planning | Position AI-assisted planning as human-reviewed organization, not automated performance. |

## Highest-Value TC Intake Questions

1. What should the MC style feel like: minimal/polished, warm/family-focused, bilingual/energetic, high-energy host, or corporate/formal?
2. How should guest requests be handled: open requests, couple-approved only, no requests, or DJ discretion?
3. What music balance should guide the night: Latin-heavy, American pop/open format, mixed family/cross-generation, club-style, clean corporate, or custom?
4. Are there must-play, do-not-play, pronunciation, dedication, or family-tradition notes?
5. Is ceremony audio in a separate space from cocktail hour or reception?
6. Is any part of the event outdoors, beachside, tented, or weather-exposed?
7. Who is the planner, venue contact, or day-of decision-maker?
